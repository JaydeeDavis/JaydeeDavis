Why the Duke is Better

The KTM Duke 390 and the BMW G 310 R are both standout options in the entry-level motorcycle market, but the Duke 390 edges ahead for several compelling reasons. With its powerful 373cc engine, the Duke 390 delivers a thrilling 44 horsepower, significantly outperforming the BMW's 313cc engine with 34 horsepower. This extra power translates to quicker acceleration and a more exhilarating ride.

Another Point

The Duke 390 also boasts advanced features like a TFT color display, which enhances rider experience with intuitive controls and clear visibility.

You Really Should Know by Now

Its aggressive styling and lightweight chassis make it a nimble and responsive bike, ideal for urban environments and twisty roads alike.

Your Bike is Slow

Additionally, the KTM's superior suspension setup ensures a smoother ride over varied terrains, adding to its versatility.

It's Basically a Scooter

For riders seeking a blend of performance, cutting-edge technology, and striking aesthetics, the Duke 390 stands as the superior choice.

Summary of Differences

Engine and Performance: The Duke 390 has a larger engine (373cc vs. 313cc) and higher power output (44 hp vs. 34 hp).

Weight: The Duke 390 is lighter (149 kg vs. 158.5 kg).

Display: The Duke 390 features a more advanced TFT display compared to the BMW's LCD display.

Fuel Capacity: The Duke 390 has a larger fuel tank (13.4 liters vs. 11 liters).

Seat Height: The Duke 390 has a higher seat height (830 mm vs. 785 mm).

These differences underscore the Duke 390's focus on performance, advanced technology, and a dynamic riding experience, making it the preferred choice for many enthusiasts.
